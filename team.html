Our Team

The core members driving innovation in intelligent energy systems, wireless power transfer, and electrified mobility.

Lab Director

Dr. Yousef A. Mahmoud

Associate Professor, Electrical & Computer Engineering
Kennesaw State University

Dr. Yousef A. Mahmoud (Senior Member, IEEE) is an Associate Professor at Kennesaw State University. He received his Ph.D. from the University of Waterloo in 2016, specializing in photovoltaic systems, power electronics, protection, and real-time modeling.

He serves as Associate Editor for the IEEE Transactions on Energy Conversion and IEEE Power Engineering Letters. His research includes solar diagnostics, fault detection, modeling of partially shaded PV systems, and highly efficient real-time PV estimators that enabled new computational-vision-based PV diagnostics.

Ph.D. Researchers

Islam A. Sayed

Ph.D. Candidate, Energy Management Systems

Islam Sayed (Graduate Student Member, IEEE) earned his B.Sc. and M.Sc. from Cairo University and is currently a Ph.D. candidate at Kennesaw State University with a Graduate Scholarship and Research Assistantship.

His work focuses on hybrid energy storage systems (battery–supercapacitor–solar), KalmanNet-based SOC/SOH estimation, multi-agent reinforcement learning EMS, and real-time PIL/HIL validation using TI C2000 and Speedgoat platforms. He was selected among the Top 20 Reviewers at IEEE ECCE 2025 and is a Full Member of Sigma Xi.

Mahmoud Houwaidi

Ph.D. Candidate, Wireless Power Transfer

Mahmoud Houwaidi is a Ph.D. researcher at the iENERGY Lab specializing in Wireless Power Transfer (WPT) for electric vehicles and advanced energy systems. His research includes high-efficiency WPT coil design, compensation networks, electromagnetic modeling, and converter control for stationary and dynamic EV wireless charging.

His work supports next-generation EV charging systems targeting higher power density, improved alignment robustness, and reliable real-time system control.
