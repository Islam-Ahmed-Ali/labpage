Our Team

The core members driving research and innovation in intelligent energy systems, hybrid energy storage, wireless power transfer, and electrified mobility.

Lab Director

Dr. Yousef A. Mahmoud

Associate Professor, Electrical & Computer Engineering
Kennesaw State University

Dr. Yousef A. Mahmoud (Senior Member, IEEE) is an Associate Professor at Kennesaw State University. He received his Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Waterloo in 2016. His research interests include photovoltaic systems, power electronics, protection and fault diagnosis, real-time system modeling, and intelligent energy conversion technologies. He currently serves as an Associate Editor for the IEEE Transactions on Energy Conversion and IEEE Power Engineering Letters. Dr. Mahmoud’s work has received international recognition, including IEEE awards, a U.S. patent, and inclusion in Stanford University’s list of the World’s Top 2% Scientists.

Ph.D. Researchers

Islam A. Sayed

Ph.D. Candidate, Electrical & Computer Engineering

Islam A. Sayed (Graduate Student Member, IEEE) earned his B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees in Electrical Engineering from Cairo University and is currently pursuing the Ph.D. degree at Kennesaw State University. His research focuses on energy management systems for electric vehicles, hybrid energy storage systems combining batteries, supercapacitors, and solar PV, state-of-charge and state-of-health estimation, and intelligent control strategies for electrified mobility. His work emphasizes real-time validation through processor-in-the-loop and hardware-in-the-loop platforms. He has authored multiple IEEE publications, serves as a reviewer for IEEE and Elsevier journals, and was recognized among the Top 20 Reviewers at IEEE ECCE 2025.

Mahmoud Houwaidi

Ph.D. Candidate, Electrical & Computer Engineering

Mahmoud Houwaidi is a Ph.D. researcher at the iENERGY Lab specializing in wireless power transfer technologies for electric vehicles and automated guided vehicles. His research interests include inductive power transfer systems, compensation network design, electromagnetic modeling, harmonic analysis, and power electronic converters for high-frequency wireless charging applications. His work targets robust, high-efficiency wireless charging systems with improved tolerance to misalignment, load variations, and real-world operating conditions.
