About the iENERGY Lab

Intelligent Energy Systems & Electrified Mobility Laboratory

Overview

The iENERGY Lab at Kennesaw State University is dedicated to advancing the future of intelligent and sustainable energy systems. We focus on the intersection of artificial intelligence, power electronics, real-time control, and hybrid energy storage technologies for next-generation electric vehicles and renewable energy systems.

Our research integrates cutting-edge approaches such as multi-agent reinforcement learning, KalmanNet-based state estimation, real-time hardware-in-the-loop (HIL/PIL) validation, and multiphysics modeling to develop high-performance and reliable energy management solutions.

Mission

Our mission is to pioneer intelligent energy technologies that enable safer, more efficient, and more sustainable electrified transportation. We strive to bridge the gap between theoretical innovation and practical implementation through rigorous research, experimental validation, and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Vision

To become a leading hub for innovation in smart energy systems and intelligent vehicle technologies. We envision a future where AI-driven control, advanced storage systems, and real-time embedded intelligence enable clean, reliable, and affordable mobility solutions worldwide.

Research Philosophy

Our work is grounded in the belief that intelligent energy systems must combine data-driven learning, physics-based modeling, and real-time embedded execution. By merging artificial intelligence with domain knowledge, the iENERGY Lab develops robust and generalizable solutions that address real-world engineering challenges.

Collaborations

The lab actively collaborates with academic institutions, industry partners, and research communities across power electronics, renewable energy, transportation electrification, and AI. We welcome opportunities for joint research, student involvement, and technology transfer.
