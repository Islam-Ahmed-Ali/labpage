Lab News & Highlights

Major research milestones, publications, recognitions, and academic activities from the iENERGY Lab.

IEEE Access Review Paper Published

2025

The comprehensive review paper “Energy Management of Battery–Supercapacitor Hybrid Storage Systems in Electric Vehicles with Solar Integration” was published in IEEE Access, summarizing state-of-the-art methods in hybrid energy storage systems, energy management strategies, and renewable energy integration.

Future Batteries Journal Publication Released

2025

The paper “Robust Hybrid Neural–Kalman Filter for Real-Time Supercapacitor State-of-Charge Estimation in Electric Vehicles” was published in Future Batteries (Elsevier, Q1), presenting real-time state estimation results under dynamic EV operating conditions.

ECCE 2025 Student Project Demo Nomination

2025

A KalmanNet-based Processor-in-the-Loop (PIL) demonstration for supercapacitor SOC estimation was nominated among the top projects in the IEEE Student Project Demonstration Competition, led by IEEE Life Fellow Dr. Muhammad H. Rashid.

ECCE 2025 Oral & Poster Presentations

2025

The iENERGY Lab presented two papers at IEEE ECCE 2025, including an oral presentation on battery State-of-Health estimation and a poster presentation on supercapacitor State-of-Charge estimation.

Top 20 Reviewer Recognition – IEEE ECCE 2025

2025

Ph.D. researcher Islam A. Sayed was recognized among the Top 20 Reviewers in the Power Converter Topologies & Control track at IEEE ECCE 2025.

Sigma Xi Full Membership

2025

Islam A. Sayed was nominated and accepted as a Full Member of Sigma Xi, the Scientific Research Honor Society, in recognition of his research contributions and scholarly achievements.

IECON 2024 Oral Presentations

2024

Two oral presentations were delivered at IEEE IECON 2024: one on battery State-of-Charge estimation and another on wireless power transfer systems for automated guided vehicles (AGVs).

KSU Fall 2024 Research Symposium

2024

The iENERGY Lab presented ongoing research on energy management systems and energy storage state estimation at the Kennesaw State University Fall Research Symposium.

Expansion of AGV Wireless Power Transfer Research

2024

A dedicated research track on wireless power transfer for automated guided vehicles (AGVs) was established, focusing on compensation networks, harmonic analysis, coupling variation, and system-level optimization.

Energy Management & State Estimation Research Established

2024

The iENERGY Lab formally established its core research direction in energy management systems and state estimation for batteries and supercapacitors in electric vehicle applications.
